opening
arr. flute, viola, cello, baritone
This was my final project for MUSC 320: Orchestration and Arranging - a four-part ensemble arrangement of Philip Glass' brilliant piano piece. The piece follows a slow-moving, simply-constructed harmonic progression carried on dutifully by the inner voices (viola, cello) accompanied by the underlying/overlying voices of flute and baritone. This orchestration allows for a dissected look at how each part functions to create beautiful homegenous sound in awesome Minmalist style. Flute part's counterpoint with baritone needs just a bit more tweaking. The higher registers called upon by this arrangement isn't quite so pleasant. But, ah a nice first draft. A big thank you to Dr. Adler, instructor of the course for guidance, direciton on writing this arrangement and conducting of first recording. And another thank you to my friends Mariana, David, Cecilia, and Jonathan who played this arrangement for the first time and to whom the parts are dedicated.
